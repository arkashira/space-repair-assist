# Business Model Canvas for Space Repair Assist

## Customer Segments
- Space mission operators and engineers (government and private sectors).
- Aerospace companies involved in satellite and spacecraft manufacturing.
- Research institutions focused on space exploration and technology.
- Educational institutions offering aerospace engineering programs.

## Value Propositions
- Remote guidance and simulation for server repair in space, reducing downtime and risk.
- On-demand 3D printing and fabrication of necessary tools or parts, enhancing mission flexibility.
- Cost-effective solution for training and operational efficiency in space missions.
- Unique offering with no direct competition in the current Axentx portfolio.

## Channels
- Direct sales through partnerships with aerospace companies and space agencies.
- Online platform for product demonstrations and virtual trials.
- Industry conferences and trade shows to showcase technology and gather feedback.
- Collaboration with educational institutions for training programs and workshops.

## Customer Relationships
- Dedicated account management for enterprise clients to ensure tailored support.
- Online community and forums for users to share experiences and solutions.
- Regular updates and maintenance support to enhance user satisfaction.
- Feedback loops for continuous improvement based on user input.

## Revenue Streams
- Subscription model for software access (estimated $500/month per user).
- Licensing fees for enterprise solutions (estimated $50,000/year).
- Revenue from training programs and workshops (estimated $10,000 per session).
- Potential revenue from partnerships with hardware manufacturers for integrated solutions.

## Key Resources
- Development team skilled in AR/VR technologies and 3D printing.
- Intellectual property related to software and hardware integration.
- Partnerships with aerospace industry leaders for insights and validation.
- Access to datasets for continuous improvement and feature enhancements.

## Key Activities
- Continuous development and enhancement of AR/VR tools and 3D printing capabilities.
- Market research to identify emerging trends and customer needs.
- Building and maintaining partnerships with key industry players.
- Marketing and outreach efforts to raise awareness and generate leads.

## Key Partners
- Aerospace companies (e.g., SpaceX, Boeing) for collaboration and validation.
- Educational institutions for research and training partnerships.
- Technology providers specializing in AR/VR and 3D printing.
- Government agencies involved in space exploration and technology funding.

## Cost Structure
- Development costs for software and hardware integration (estimated $1,000,000/year).
- Marketing and sales expenses (estimated $200,000/year).
- Operational costs including customer support and maintenance (estimated $150,000/year).
- Partnership and collaboration expenses (estimated $100,000/year).
